Seismic Retrofit

Seismic retrofit revolves around reinforcing buildings to withstand earthquakes. With it, both the interior and exterior of a building will move as one rather than separately, ultimately preserving the overall structure.

As we have seen in earthquakes throughout the years, some homes hold up better than others. Each building is different, and it all comes down to one major factor -how they were originally built.

Those who own older homes (particularly those built on raised foundations) are susceptible to earthquake damage. However, homes constructed after the year 1985 were built to stricter standards. Those with older homes must ensure they carry out seismic retrofit to ensure their home will survive a seismic event.
